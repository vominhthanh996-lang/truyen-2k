# Revision Checklist

Use this before calling a chapter polished.

## 1. Scene, Not Summary

- Does the chapter contain at least one dramatized scene with location, bodies, objects, and dialogue?
- Are important decisions shown in action instead of explained after the fact?
- Can the reader feel where everyone is standing?

## 2. Human Cost

- What does the protagonist lose, risk, owe, or misunderstand?
- Who is unhappy with the protagonist's choice?
- Does a saved person still have consequences, trauma, debt, or agency?

## 3. Survival Logic

- Track water, food, medicine, radiation, tools, labor, and information.
- If something valuable appears, ask: who wants it, who controls it, who pays to keep it?
- If the team helps someone, ask: what does that cost tonight?

## 4. Dialogue Test

Cover the speaker names. Can you still tell who said each line?

If not:

- make Tần Dã shorter and more tactical;
- make A Thất sharper and more talkative;
- make Di Mạn scolding but useful;
- make Lâm Tịch direct, tired, and increasingly strategic;
- make bureaucrats precise, calm, and dangerous.

## 5. Paragraph Rhythm

For webnovel readability:

- short paragraphs for tension;
- medium paragraphs for sensory/emotional grounding;
- avoid walls of exposition;
- place a clean emotional line after a complex action scene.
- for audio, prefer 30-50 short/medium paragraphs per chapter instead of a few dense blocks;
- after two explanatory paragraphs, return to a hand, mouth, tool, wound, cup, door, bag, or other visible object;
- use inner questions to create pull, but answer them through action whenever possible.

## 5.5. Văn Người Test

Before approving a chapter, ask:

- Is anyone hungry, thirsty, tired, embarrassed, afraid, or quietly relieved on the page?
- Did a character do one small caring thing without announcing it as care?
- Does the chapter contain a practical worry that ordinary people understand: price, queue, debt, smell, dirt, pain, sleep, cold, medicine?
- Can the emotional beat be filmed in one shot: a cup left untouched, a hand stopping midair, a bowl wiped twice, a blanket folded, a door not locked?
- If all lore terms are removed, does the scene still hurt or feel warm?

## 6. "Sảng" Without Breaking Tone

Satisfying moments should come from:

- standing up after being looked down on;
- using survival knowledge to beat a stronger enemy;
- protecting a rule the team chose;
- turning a bureaucratic trap back on the system;
- earning public trust through visible cost.
- finding a small object that changes tonight's meal, warmth, safety, or bargaining power;
- making a "useless" mutated thing useful through knowledge, patience, or risk;
- reaching a material milestone the audience can count: one more cup of clean water, one more stable dose, one more crystal, one more dry bed.

Avoid easy power fantasy. This story's pleasure is "weak people becoming hard to swallow."

## 7. Final Hook

The ending should answer one small question and open one stronger question.

Examples:

- They get water, but now everyone knows they control a valve.
- Tần Dã walks farther, but Hắc Nha notices.
- A child survives, but the law now wants custody.
- The city gives a card, but the card is a chain.
